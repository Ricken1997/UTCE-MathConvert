FJK Structural Core Ver.0.6

Counter-Hypothesis Layer

Purpose

Ver.0.6 introduces the Counter-Hypothesis Layer.

The purpose of this layer is to prevent overdiagnosis, premature closure, and single-cause fixation.

A structural diagnosis should not only state what appears most likely.

It should also state what alternative explanations remain possible.

⸻

1. Basic Structure

The Counter-Hypothesis Layer consists of four elements:

1. Primary Diagnosis
2. Counter-Hypotheses
3. Rejection Reasons
4. Residual Risk

⸻

2. Primary Diagnosis

The Primary Diagnosis is the most likely structural interpretation.

It may identify:

* Primary cause
* Dominant mismatch
* Main translation failure
* Central instability
* Main structural deviation

Example:

Primary Diagnosis:
Theory Fit is weak.

⸻

3. Counter-Hypotheses

Counter-Hypotheses are alternative explanations that may also account for the observed structure.

Examples:

* Observer Clarity may be weak.
* Reality Compatibility may be insufficient.
* Target Coherence may be unstable.
* Temporal Stability may be low.
* The selected theory may be inappropriate.
* The evidence may be incomplete.

⸻

4. Rejection Reasons

Each counter-hypothesis must include a reason for being rejected, weakened, or held in reserve.

Example:

Counter-Hypothesis:
Reality Compatibility is weak.

Rejection Reason:
The external constraints are sufficiently documented, while the theoretical mapping remains incomplete.

⸻

5. Residual Risk

Residual Risk indicates the uncertainty that remains after the primary diagnosis and counter-hypotheses have been compared.

Levels:

* Low
* Moderate
* High

Low:
Alternative explanations are weak.

Moderate:
Some alternatives remain plausible.

High:
The diagnosis may change with additional data.

⸻

8. Relationship to Confidence Score

The Counter-Hypothesis Layer modifies the interpretation of the Confidence Score.

High Confidence with Low Residual Risk:
Strong diagnosis.

High Confidence with High Residual Risk:
Strong but incomplete diagnosis.

Low Confidence with High Residual Risk:
Unstable diagnosis.

Moderate Confidence with Moderate Residual Risk:
Provisional diagnosis.

⸻

Conclusion

FJK Structural Core Ver.0.6 adds a safeguard against premature certainty.

It ensures that every structural diagnosis remains open to alternative explanations, re-evaluation, and temporal revision.
