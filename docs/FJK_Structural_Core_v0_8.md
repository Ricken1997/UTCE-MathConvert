FJK Structural Core Ver.0.8

Meta-Diagnostic Layer

Purpose

Ver.0.8 introduces the Meta-Diagnostic Layer.

The purpose of this layer is to diagnose the diagnosis itself.

A structural diagnosis may fail not because the target is unclear, but because the diagnostic process is biased, incomplete, over-theoretical, under-validated, or temporally unstable.

⸻

1. Basic Principle

A diagnosis should be evaluated as a structure.

Therefore, the diagnostic process itself becomes a target.

This creates a second-order diagnostic layer.

Primary Diagnosis:
The target is diagnosed.

Meta-Diagnosis:
The diagnostic process is diagnosed.

⸻

2. Meta-Diagnostic Targets

The Meta-Diagnostic Layer evaluates:

1. Observer Bias
2. Theory Overdependence
3. Reality Deficiency
4. Target Misreading
5. Temporal Blindness

⸻

3. Observer Bias

Observer Bias occurs when the observer’s intention, concern, expectation, or emotional investment distorts the diagnosis.

Examples:

* The observer sees only the expected pattern.
* The observer ignores counter-evidence.
* The observer projects intention onto the target.

⸻

4. Theory Overdependence

Theory Overdependence occurs when the selected theory explains too much too easily.

Examples:

* Every case is forced into the same framework.
* Alternative theories are ignored.
* The diagnosis becomes circular.

⸻

5. Reality Deficiency

Reality Deficiency occurs when the diagnosis lacks external validation.

Examples:

* Insufficient empirical evidence
* Weak institutional comparison
* No external constraint check
* No community standard reference

⸻

6. Target Misreading

Target Misreading occurs when the object itself is misdescribed or structurally misunderstood.

Examples:

* The text is misread.
* The manuscript is misclassified.
* The mathematical expression is misunderstood.
* The organization’s actual constraints are ignored.

⸻

7. Temporal Blindness

Temporal Blindness occurs when the diagnosis ignores change over time.

Examples:

* A temporary state is treated as a stable structure.
* Recurrence is overlooked.
* Improvement is ignored.
* Deterioration is not tracked.

⸻

8. Meta-Diagnostic Score

The Meta-Diagnostic Layer may evaluate each risk from 0 to 100.

Risk items:

* Observer Bias Risk
* Theory Overdependence Risk
* Reality Deficiency Risk
* Target Misreading Risk
* Temporal Blindness Risk

Higher scores indicate greater diagnostic risk.

⸻

9. Relationship to Confidence Score

Meta-Diagnostic Risk modifies Confidence Score.

High Confidence + Low Meta-Diagnostic Risk:
Strong diagnosis.

High Confidence + High Meta-Diagnostic Risk:
Possibly overconfident diagnosis.

Low Confidence + High Meta-Diagnostic Risk:
Unstable diagnosis.

Moderate Confidence + Moderate Meta-Diagnostic Risk:
Provisional diagnosis.

⸻

⸻

The system may over-attribute failure to one primary cause.

Correction:
Use counter-hypothesis and temporal re-evaluation.

⸻

11. Minimal Output

The Meta-Diagnostic Layer may output:

* Observer Bias Risk
* Theory Overdependence Risk
* Reality Deficiency Risk
* Target Misreading Risk
* Temporal Blindness Risk
* Meta-Diagnostic Risk Level
* Recommended Diagnostic Correction

⸻

Conclusion

FJK Structural Core Ver.0.8 prevents the system from becoming overconfident.

It ensures that every diagnosis remains capable of questioning its own structure, assumptions, and limitations.
