2. Predictive Risk Layer

Ver.1.0 introduces the Predictive Risk Layer.

This layer estimates the probability that a structure will fail, recur, destabilize, or require intervention.

⸻

3. Predictive Risk Components

Predictive Risk is based on five components:

1. Low Confidence
2. High Residual Risk
3. Temporal Instability
4. Meta-Diagnostic Risk
5. Weak Cross-Application Validation

⸻

4. Minimal Predictive Risk Formula

Let:

CS = Confidence Score
RR = Residual Risk
TI = Temporal Instability
MDR = Meta-Diagnostic Risk
CAV = Cross-Application Validation Score

Then:

Predictive Risk = (100 − CS + RR + TI + MDR + (100 − CAV)) / 5

Range:

0–100

Interpretation:

0–19:
Minimal Risk

20–39:
Low Risk

40–59:
Moderate Risk

60–79:
High Risk

80–100:
Critical Risk
